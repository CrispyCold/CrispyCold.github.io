⚡ Why You Miss Entries

Most beginners see a token pumping and jump in — but by then the early buyers are already selling to you. The key is to catch tokens before they pump.

Use the Entry Sniper tab. It finds tokens with early momentum: not yet pumped big, but showing buy pressure building. That's your window.

🚦 What the Signals Mean

🟢 ENTRY ZONE — Early momentum building, buy pressure dominant, not yet pumped. This is your window.

🟡 WAIT — Mixed signals. Could still go, but not clear yet. Watch, don't rush in.

🔴 AVOID — Already pumped a lot, or too risky (low liquidity, high sell pressure). Easy to get rekt.

💡 Beginner Rules to Live By

Only risk what you can lose. Start with tiny amounts while you learn.

Set a stop-loss mentally. If a trade goes down 30%, exit. Don't hold bags hoping it recovers.

Don't chase green candles. If a token is already up 200%, you're late. Wait for the next one.

ENTRY CHECKLIST — Run Through This Before Every Trade
✅ Pre-Trade Checklist
01
Is the liquidity above $10,000? Below this and you may not be able to sell without huge slippage. Check the LIQ number on each card.
02
Are buys outnumbering sells? Look at the B/S ratio. If buys are 2x+ higher than sells, people are accumulating. Good sign.
03
Is it an "Early" or "Mid" entry on the curve? Every token shows where it is on the price curve. "Early" = best. "Topped" = skip it.
04
Is the 5-minute price change fresh? A token moving +10% or more in the last 5 mins with volume is active right now.
05
Did you check the chart? Always open FOMO App and look at the chart before buying. A healthy chart shows steady climbing candles, not one giant spike that's already over.
06
Have you decided your exit in advance? Know before you buy: "I'll sell half at +50%, rest at +100%, and exit completely if it drops 30%." Write it down if you have to.
GLOSSARY — Plain English Definitions
📖 What Does That Mean?
Market Cap (MCap)
The total value of all coins in existence. Under $500K = tiny, easy to pump. Over $10M = harder to move. Lower MCap = more risk, more reward.
Liquidity (LIQ)
The money available for buying/selling in the pool. Higher is better. Under $5K = you can barely sell without crashing the price.
B/S Ratio
Buys divided by Sells. A ratio above 1.5 means way more people are buying than selling — bullish. Below 0.8 means selling pressure — bearish.
Momentum Score
A 0-100 score combining price speed, volume, buy pressure, and liquidity. 70+ = strong momentum. 40 or below = fading or weak.
Rug Pull
When the developer drains the liquidity pool and runs, leaving you with worthless tokens. Signs: very low liquidity, token is brand new, no socials. Avoid.
Volume
How much $ has been traded in the last hour. $50K+ is active. Low volume = nobody's trading it, which means slow buys and hard sells.
Entry Curve
Where on the price journey a token appears to be. Early = just starting. Mid = in the middle of its pump. Late = likely near the top. Topped = probably done pumping.
Slippage
The difference between the price you expect and the price you actually get. High slippage happens when liquidity is low — you buy at a worse price than shown.
Whale
A wallet with a lot of SOL (usually 100+ SOL). When whales buy, prices often move up. When they sell, it can crash fast.
Smart Money
Wallets that consistently enter tokens early and exit at highs. If you see smart money buying a token, that's a positive signal worth noting.
TRADING TIPS FOR BEGINNERS
💡 TIP #1 — The 2-Minute Rule

When you spot a token, give yourself exactly 2 minutes to decide. If you can't answer "Why am I buying this?" in 2 minutes, skip it. Hesitation usually means it's not the right setup.

💡 TIP #2 — Never Go All-In

Split your trade budget into 5-10 equal parts. Use only 1 part per trade. This way, losing one trade doesn't wipe you out and you get to practice many more times.

💡 TIP #3 — Watch the 5-Minute Chart First

In FOMO App, always look at the 5-minute candles before buying. You want to see consistent green candles with volume — not one giant spike that happened an hour ago.

💡 TIP #4 — Refresh Every 2-3 Minutes

The best setups move fast. Hit Refresh Now at the top of this page every 2-3 minutes when you're actively hunting entries. The countdown timer tells you when auto-refresh fires.
